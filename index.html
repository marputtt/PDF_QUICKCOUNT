List of The Parties

Here are the list of parties that participate in PEMILU 2024 in Indonesia 

Focus on the big picture

Golkar is officially known as the Party of Functional Groups (Partai Golongan Karya), is a prominent political party in Indonesia. Established in 1964, it initially aimed to represent various economic and social groups in the country.

The party has a history of supporting policies focused on economic development and has been a significant political player, often part of the ruling coalition. Golkar's influence has persisted, despite facing internal divisions and challenges.

Focus on the big picture

The Indonesian Democratic Party of Struggle (PDI-P) is a prominent secular-nationalist political party in Indonesia. Led by Megawati Sukarnoputri, the daughter of Indonesia's first president, Sukarno, the party aims to realize the goals of the Indonesian Constitution.

It has a strong voter base and represents marginalized communities, advocating for nationalist values and economic nationalism. Despite controversies, it remains a frontrunner in pre-election surveys, aiming for consecutive electoral victories.

Focus on the big picture

Partai Amanat Nasional (PAN) is an Islam-based political party in Indonesia, founded on August 23, 1998, by the modernist strand of Muslim society in Indonesia, including Amien Rais, the chairman of the Muhammadiyah organization, during the Indonesian revolution.

The party's current leader is Zulkifli Hasan, who was re-elected as PAN General Chairman for the 2020-2025 term. PAN's ideology is Islamic democracy, religious nationalism, and Pancasila.

Focus on the big picture

The Nasdem Party (Partai NasDem) is a political party in Indonesia, founded in 2011 by media baron Surya Paloh. It is partly funded by Paloh and has a membership of 395,949. The party's ideology is based on Pancasila, Indonesian nationalism, secularism, and social liberalism.

It is positioned at the center to center-left of the political spectrum and is affiliated with the Onward Indonesia Coalition and the Change Coalition. Surya Paloh is the party's General Chairman, and the party has 59 seats in the DPR

Gerindra is a right-wing populist political party in Indonesia, established in 2008 by Prabowo Subianto, a former general and businessman. The party's policy platform remains hazy and at times contradictory, but it follows a populist and nationalist economic platform, targeting the lower middle class such as farmers and fishers. Gerindra has substantial financial resources provided by Prabowo's brother, Hashim Djojohadikusomo, one of Indonesia's richest men.

For the 2020-2025 term, the party is led by Prabowo Subianto as its chairman, assisted by Sufmi Dasco Ahmad as the central executive board head, Ahmad Muzani as the secretary-general, and Thomas Mulyatni Djiwandono as the treasurer
